Frequently Asked Questions
Q1: Is it worth replacing a Land Rover engine?
It depends entirely on vehicle value versus replacement cost. A Range Rover Sport or Defender L663 will almost always justify replacement — the car is worth far more than the engine. A Freelander 2 or early Discovery Sport often won't; replacement cost can exceed what the car is worth outright. Use our diagnostic calculator above to get a personalised verdict.

Q2: Which Land Rover engine should I avoid?
The 2.7 TDV6 (276DT) and the 3.0 TDV6/SDV6 (306DT) carry the highest catastrophic-failure risk in the range — both can suffer bottom-end failures that leave little to rebuild. The 4.4 SDV8 and post-2019 Ingenium units are considerably more dependable. See our Engine Rankings for the full breakdown.

Q3: Is the Ingenium engine reliable?
The petrol Ingenium (204PT) has a noticeably better reliability record than the diesel (204DTD), which suffered timing chain tensioner issues on pre-2019 builds. JLR revised the design from 2019 onward, and those engines carry meaningfully lower risk. Check our Ingenium Knowledge Centre for full details.

Q4: Discovery 4 vs Discovery 5 — which is more reliable?
The Discovery 5 benefits from the revised, more dependable Ingenium engines and a lighter aluminium platform. The Discovery 4 is more readily available on the SDV6, which is powerful but carries real crankshaft bearing risk at higher mileages — service history matters more than the badge. Read our full comparison here.

Q5: How do I know if my Land Rover is worth repairing?
Compare your realistic vehicle value against the full replacement quote, not just the repair estimate. As a rule, if replacement cost sits above roughly 60% of what the car is worth, it's worth getting a scrap valuation before committing. Use our Ownership Decision Tool for a personalised assessment.

Q6: Which is the safest Range Rover to buy used?
The L405 (2012–2021) offers the widest margin between typical resale value and replacement cost of any generation, which is why it tops our safest-to-buy ranking. The newer L460 is even safer on paper, but at a significantly higher entry price. See our full Range Rover Rankings for details.
